# 👗 Zara Sales Analysis Dashboard
## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes Zara's global sales performance across product categories, seasons, materials, and sourcing countries to identify revenue drivers and optimize inventory and promotional strategies. The dashboard provides insights into total revenue, units sold, product-level performance, seasonal trends, and country-of-origin distribution.

The goal is to enable retail managers and merchandising teams to make data-driven decisions around product planning, seasonal campaigns, and supply chain sourcing.

## 🎯 Business Objective

• Identify which product sections (Men vs Women) and categories drive the highest revenue

• Analyze how seasons impact revenue and quantity sold across product terms

• Evaluate the effectiveness of promotions on overall sales performance

• Compare product material popularity to guide future inventory decisions

• Understand country-of-origin distribution to assess supply chain diversity

## 📈 Dashboard Insights
### 1️⃣ Section Performance

• The Women's section dominates revenue at $598.49M — more than double the Men's section at $286.11M

• This indicates Zara's core customer base and primary revenue engine is women's fashion

• Men's section represents a significant growth opportunity if product range is expanded

### 2️⃣ Seasonal Revenue Trends

• Autumn is the highest revenue season at $308.04M, followed by Winter at $250.23M

• Spring generates $183.32M while Summer is the weakest season at $143.01M

• This suggests heavier inventory investment in Autumn/Winter collections would yield better returns

• Jackets are the top-earning product term across all seasons, significantly outperforming Sweaters, T-Shirts, Shoes, and Jeans

### 3️⃣ Material Performance

• Cotton leads all materials with 3,851 units, closely followed by Wool at 3,805 units

• Wool Blend (3,373), Polyester (2,775), and Linen (2,573) round out the top 5 materials

• Premium materials like Silk (38) and Satin (132) have very low volumes — likely positioned as high-margin niche products

• Cotton and Wool dominance suggests customer preference for natural fabrics

### 4️⃣ Promotion Impact

• Promoted products generated $440.71M vs $443.89M for non-promoted products — an almost identical split

• This suggests promotions are not significantly outperforming regular pricing, warranting a review of the current promotion strategy

• 53.63% of units were sold under promotion, meaning over half the catalog is discounted — a potential margin risk

### 5️⃣ Seasonal Product Mix

• 53.33% of products are tagged as seasonal, generating $458.40M vs $426.20M for non-seasonal items

• Seasonal products hold a slight revenue edge, validating the seasonal collection strategy

• However, the gap is narrow — non-seasonal staples remain a strong revenue contributor

### 6️⃣ Country of Origin

• China and Bangladesh are the top two sourcing countries, dominating product origin by volume

• Turkey and India follow as significant manufacturing hubs

• Morocco, Portugal, Spain, Vietnam, and Cambodia represent mid-tier sourcing countries

• Pakistan and Argentina have minimal product volume — likely used for specialized or limited product lines

• Heavy reliance on China and Bangladesh presents a supply chain concentration risk

## 💡 Business Recommendations

• Double down on Autumn and Winter collections — they account for over 63% of total seasonal revenue

• Prioritize Women's section product expansion while developing targeted Men's campaigns to close the revenue gap

• Re-evaluate promotion strategy — nearly equal revenue between promoted and non-promoted items suggests discounts may be cannibalizing margins without driving incremental volume

• Expand Cotton and Wool-based product lines as they clearly lead customer preference by volume

• Diversify the supply chain beyond China and Bangladesh to reduce geopolitical and logistics risk

• Develop Summer-specific product lines or marketing pushes to address the weakest revenue season at $143.01M
