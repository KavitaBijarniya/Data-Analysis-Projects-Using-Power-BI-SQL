# 📦 Product Sales Analysis Dashboard Using Excel
## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes product sales performance across multiple categories, regions, cities, and customer segments to uncover revenue trends, top-performing products, and regional demand patterns. The dashboard features a dynamic measure selector allowing stakeholders to switch between Total Orders, Total Profit, Total Quantity, and Total Revenue across all visuals simultaneously.

The goal is to enable sales managers and business leaders to identify growth opportunities, optimize product portfolios, and allocate resources to the highest-performing regions and customer segments.

## 🎯 Business Objective

• Track overall sales performance across revenue, profit, quantity, and order volume

• Identify which product categories, sub-categories, and individual products drive the most orders

• Analyze regional and city-level demand to guide distribution and marketing strategies

• Understand monthly order trends to detect seasonality and peak demand periods

• Identify top customers to support key account management and loyalty programs

## 📈 Dashboard Insights
### 1️⃣ Monthly Sales Trend

• Orders were consistently low from January through August, staying between 5,000 and 15,000 per month

• A sharp and sustained growth spike begins in September, peaking in November at nearly 40,000 orders

• December shows a slight pullback to around 28,000 orders but remains far above mid-year levels

• This strong Q4 seasonality signals a heavy dependence on holiday-driven demand — October through December accounts for the majority of annual volume

### 2️⃣ Performance by Category

• Electronics leads all categories with 62,298 total orders — the single largest category

• Home & Furniture follows with 51,230 orders

• Accessories (50,564) and Clothing & Apparel (35,908) round out the category mix

• Electronics and Home & Furniture together account for over 56% of all orders

### 3️⃣ Regional Performance

• East region leads in total orders, closely followed by West

• Centre and South regions trail behind with South being the weakest region

• The East-West dominance suggests stronger urban retail infrastructure or customer density in those regions

• South region represents the clearest geographic growth opportunity

### 4️⃣ Top 20 Product Performance

• Phone Case and Instant Pot are among the highest-ordered individual products

• Tempur-Pedic Mattress, Adidas Tracksuit, and Nike Running Shoes show strong mid-tier performance

• iPad Pro, KitchenAid Mixer, and Apple Watch perform consistently across the top 20

• Lower-ranked products like Under Armour T-Shirt and Closet Organizer may warrant promotion support or inventory review

### 5️⃣ Sub-Category Performance

• Men's Wear and Laptops are among the strongest sub-categories by order volume

• Footwear, Kids Wear, and Sportswear also show healthy order counts

• Bags and Bedding sit at the bottom of sub-category performance, suggesting either low demand or limited product range

• Wearables and Wearable Accessories show moderate volumes — a growing segment worth monitoring given market trends

### 6️⃣ Top 10 Customers

• Michael Smith is the single highest-ordering customer with nearly 100 orders — significantly ahead of all others

• Michael Johnson ranks second with approximately 78 orders

• The top 10 customers (all with the surname Smith, Johnson, or Williams) represent a concentrated high-value segment

• A key account management or loyalty reward strategy targeting these customers could protect and grow this revenue base

### 7️⃣ Top 10 Cities

• Rochester leads all cities in total orders at nearly 480,000, followed closely by Jersey City at approximately 475,000

• Manchester, Burlington, and New Haven form a strong mid-tier cluster

• Hartford, Pittsburgh, and Wilmington are the lowest-performing cities among the top 10

• Relatively tight order volumes across all 10 cities suggest fairly even geographic demand distribution with modest concentration at the top

## 💡 Business Recommendations

• Capitalize on Q4 seasonality with pre-stocked inventory and targeted campaigns starting in September — the October to November surge is the single biggest revenue window

• Invest in South region expansion through localized marketing and distribution partnerships to close the gap with East and West regions

• Develop a VIP loyalty program for top customers like Michael Smith and Michael Johnson — high-frequency buyers are the most cost-efficient revenue source to protect

• Prioritize Electronics and Home & Furniture inventory planning as they collectively drive over half of all orders

• Review Bags and Bedding sub-categories for either product range expansion or promotional pushes to lift their low order volumes

• Use the dynamic measure selector to run monthly profitability reviews — switching between Revenue and Profit views can reveal products with high orders but low margin contribution

## 🛠 Tools Used

• Microsoft Excel (Dashboard Development, Pivot Tables & Data Visualization)

• Power Query (Data Cleaning & Transformation)

• Excel Charts & Slicers (Interactive Filtering & Dynamic Measure Selector)

• Excel Formulas & Named Ranges (KPI Calculations)
